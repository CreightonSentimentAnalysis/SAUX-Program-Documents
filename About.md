This research explores the use of LLMs for providing quantitative zero-shot sentiment analysis of implicit software desirability, 
addressing a critical challenge in product evaluation where traditional review scores fail to capture the richness of qualitative user feedback. 

Innovations include establishing a method that:

1) works with qualitative user experience data without the need for explicit review scores,
2) focuses on implicit user satisfaction, and 3) provides scaled numerical sentiment analysis, offering a more nuanced understanding of user sentiment,
   instead of simply classifying sentiment as positive, neutral, or negative.
3) This research also explores trade-offs between cost and performance in LLMs by comparing LLMs such as GPT-4o with their much smaller and more cost effective alternatives, such as GPT-4o-mini.
4) Primarily data from the Microsoft Product Desirability Toolkit (PDT) is used and analyzed by the models, with performance measured via sentiment alignment, textual diversity, and data generation cost.  Our work shows that GPT-4o-mini achieved statistically significant sentiment scoring while maintaining a significantly lower computational cost than GPT-4o.
